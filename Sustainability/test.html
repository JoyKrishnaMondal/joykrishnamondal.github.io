Calculating Abatement Potential

All sublevers in the SMARTer2020 report smarter2020 use a common model for calculating abatement potential

y = \\alpha x

Where

  • y is the abatement potential in units of GtCO2e.
  • x is the addressable emissions in units of GtCO2e.
  • \\alpha is the saving potential as a percentage.
The way this calculation is done is fairly transparent in the SMARTer2020 report smarter2020 however they have not specified how they got the values for x and \\alpha in their report. For some sublevers it has been possible to find the values for x, \\alpha or both given the source.

Calculating Abatement potential using Life Cycle Assesment

The effect of e-commerce on reducing CO2 emission has been studied using Life Cycle Assessment lca. The conclusion from the study done tells us on average there is a 30% reduction in CO2 emission compared to traditional retail store. The study uses Monte Carlo simulation using scenario analysis for their model.

lastmile taken from their study is an interesting graph to study. It tells us some important facts:

  1. Last Mile Delivery creates significantly less CO2e emission compared to Customer Transport - by about 1.35 Kg/item. This means just by switching to an online retailer we reduce our CO2e emission by a significant margin.
  2. A fair assumption can be made that last mile delivery and warehousing for e-commerce are all linearly related to the distance between the customer and warehouse. So the model does suggest that the best way to reduce CO2e emissions further than that offered by just switching to e-commerce would be to reduce the CO2e emissions from road transportation in general. What this means from a public policy perspective is that the best way to help e-commerce companies reduce their emissions would be to invest in energy efficiency in cars or to design cities with more concentrate logistics cores, verses investing in making data centers more energy efficient as it only accounts for a much smaller part of their overall CO2e emissions.
  3. The model has a large variance because its impossible to predict what a customer might buy, increasing the uncertainty of our model, but we can make a fair assumption that for large enough sample size the variance would cancel out errors, thus as e-commerce becomes more widely adopted this model would get more accurate, which is not the case for many models that use an empirical datasets or top-down approach for finding their abatement potential as it is only accurate for past trends.

Tele-commuting is something that is fairly common in the IT industry however it has poised to become much more common in the rest of the economy futuretele. Already 1 in 5 workers telecomunicate at least 10% everyday tele. Apps like Trello trello have made is very convinient for even large organizations which traditionally prefered a centralized working environment to start having decentralized work networks.

Globalization also plays a key role is pushing tele-communting as a serious alternative to traditional office time work. Work is no more limited to 9-5 but business happens all the time throughout the world in all the different time-zones. Tele-commuting offers businesses the opportunity to keep there offices "open" unofficially after work hours.

As the IEA statistics IEA show transportation is responsible for 22% of our CO 2 e emission globally. This number is poised to grow as suburbia and other cities grow in size specially in developing countries suburbs. Also if fuel cost stays cheap like it is as of this writing of the report oil. The cost of living further from your work place will become much more affordable for the average middle class person. Given all this information - slowing down the adoption of telecommuting is going to make is much more harder to effectively control CO 2 e emissions.

From the policy prespective it should be made more easy for telecomunicating workers to obtain the same legal rights and status as their counterparts working in a cubicle. Some stricts but controvertial policies may accelerate the growth of telecommunicating even further, namely increasing the price of living near large city centers. However there is a lot of uncertainity with such policies and it might actually increase traffic jams and pollution in the short term. A much more subtle approach would be to give benefits to workers from staying off the road for a few days a week. By giving incentives to workers to not travel to work it would make it much more acceptable in the offices to not come at the office for everyday of the week.

Integration of renewables in commercial and residential buildings

There is a fundamental physics law that I always use to think aobut local energy sources and that is that of power loss in long distance transmission. The phenomenon is called ohmic heating which basically means that long distance transmission of power is unfeasable ( example generating electricity in the sahara desert and powering europe ) within our current framework of physics and engineering.

Local power sources are always more efficient due to this than centeralized grids. A great example of how significant such a technology is to look at Bangladesh. Already by 2010 8.4 million people are using solar as to power their homes bangla in Bangladesh. It has been striking to have seen such a revolution by myself. Solar power is well integrated into people's home. Because of the simple design of homes in Bangladesh it has proven non-trival to install solar panels in rooftops. Such integrated low power systems is also much more sensible for usage in developing countries verses developed countries who usually have significantly higher demand in electricity. For developed countries however the opportunity exists in the cutting edge of ICT which is the concept of "Internet of things". These are low powered devices that would surround every person's home. It does not make much sense to have wires connecting all these devices, rather these devices could be powered by solar, ambient heat, etc. These types of systems also prevent the proliferation of vampire power. Vampire power is caused by electrical leaking from devices that are technically switched off. A study in 2004 found that about 9 kWh of energy per year per household is wasted on electrical leaking of such kind vampire. These types of saving may be small but they do add up and represent low hanging friuts when it comes to reducing CO 2 e emissions.

Such Systems also provide consumers an added incentive. Rather than be passive participtors in taking care of their local elecrical grid. They now have positive incentive to reduce their power consumption and contribute to the grid. Decentralizing the power grid by allowing commercial and residential building to have renewable energy embedded in them also has the added advantage of not requiring the maintaince of expensive sub-stations and transformers. Transformers for example have non-negligible loss due to eddy current in them when switching voltage.

From a policy prespective the best way to enhance its development is to allow a a more open energy market. As of right most of the energy markets in North American and Europe are controlled by large commododized private monopolies that ditate the price of energy. By allowing each household to take control of the source of their energy we will make is much more easier to stir up innovation and generate the interest of investors.

Deforestation Prevention

RainForests are one of our largest carbon sinks. The only sure way to get carbon from the atmosphere onto the ground is through rainforests. Some develped counties like norway and sweden are actually now paying less developed countries pay to prevent from cutting down rainforest Norway. This type of action is really powerful in the short-term but sets a very wrong precedence in the long run. It allows certain countries to hold other countries 'climate hostage'.

.

The reason why such routes to fighing climate change is actually working is mainly due to development in ICT that has enabled many large countires to essential keep survellience over other less developed countries using satellite. This again in my opinion sets the wrong precedence since it seems one country is indirectly in charge of another's country soverneignity.

A better approach would be create policies that would make it easy for developing countries to monotize greatly from keeping their rainforests intact and preserved. The most simplest of such investment would be in tourism and medical research - as rainforests represent the majority of diversity of life.

An ICT enabled solution would be virtualized real-time rainforests for people to enjoy. These types of solutions are not only more effective in the long term but also greatly improves awarenesss of climate change in the general public. Creating Market oppurtunities for business to reap benefit from a certain aciton the governtment wants to be done is nothing new. The only potential problem is corruption, here ICT can be effectively used to track the flow of money and investment to prevent theft of any kind.

Conclusion

Overall the SMARTer2020 report does a decent reporting on the current trends in ICT and what it means for Climate Change. It also to some extent does try to qunatify those effects in an meaningful way.

My biggest concern from the report is the lack of good sourcing for a lot of data points that they provided us with. Many of the sub-levers I had to study had data points that just stated N/A with no furthe explanation as to why that was the case.

Also there hasn't been any examples of uncertainity analysis in their report - for example error bars, maximum and minimum estiatmation. It is a bold claim to make that the values they have found have no uncertainity associated with it.

Some other things that I think the report fails to address is how ICT is enabling change in other domains to indirectly tackle climate change. One of the main reason why developing countries are a cause of concern has to do the income disparity between rich and poor nations. ICT has enabled this to change giving people from poorer nations access to world class education and job opportunities. These types of things help to tackle one of the more fundamental problem with trying to negotiage a worldwide consesus on climate change. Many of the problems associated with climate change stems from people being desperate. Tackling this desperating caused by inequality is as crutial to solving climate change as the improvement in technology. There are many linked human related problems to Climate change. The problem of proverty, overpopulation, etc. ICT is helping to solve all these other problems that are directly feeding into help tackle the problem of climate change. Qunatifying this would be a difficult and interesting challeage but should be possible with enought data.

EarthImage is an iconic picture published during the 1980s which captures the essence of what it would require to solve the complex problem of climate change. It makes it apparent to the fact that their are no border between countries, It also captures the fact that every human regardless of social or economic status is enclosed in a shared 3D sphere.

Climate change is a problem that cannot be solved by one individual, nation or even a sub-collection of nations. It requires active participation at every level of human organization. The fastest way to get there in my opinion is just awareness to the fact that the conditions and actions of every one of us has a direct effect on the rest of us. From the reference frame of EarthImage the earth looks more like a fragile spaceship, and as far as our knowledge goes there is nothing similar to it for trillions of miles around us that we can just "relocate" to if things do not work out.
