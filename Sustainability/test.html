Calculating Abatement Potential

All sublevers in the SMARTer2020 report smarter2020 use a common model for calculating abatement potential

y = \\alpha x Where
  • y is the abatement potential in units of GtCO2e.
  • x is the addressable emissions in units of GtCO2e.
  • \\alpha is the saving potential as a percentage.
The way this calculation is done is fairly transparent in the SMARTer2020 report smarter2020 however they have not specified how they got the values for x and \\alpha in their report. For some sublevers it has been possible to find the values for x, \\alpha or both given the source.

Calculating Abatement potential using Life Cycle Assesment

The effect of e-commerce on reducing CO2 emission has been studied using Life Cycle Assessment lca. The conclusion from the study done tells us on average there is a 30% reduction in CO2 emission compared to traditional retail store. The study uses Monte Carlo simulation using scenario analysis for their model.

lastmile taken from their study is an interesting graph to study. It tells us some important facts:

  1. Last Mile Delivery creates significantly less CO2e emission compared to Customer Transport - by about 1.35 Kg/item. This means just by switching to an online retailer we reduce our CO2e emission by a significant margin.
  2. A fair assumption can be made that last mile delivery and warehousing for e-commerce are all linearly related to the distance between the customer and warehouse. So the model does suggest that the best way to reduce CO2e emissions further than that offered by just switching to e-commerce would be to reduce the CO2e emissions from road transportation in general. What this means from a public policy perspective is that the best way to help e-commerce companies reduce their emissions would be to invest in energy efficiency in cars or to design cities with more concentrate logistics cores, verses investing in making data centers more energy efficient as it only accounts for a much smaller part of their overall CO2e emissions.
  3. The model has a large variance because its impossible to predict what a customer might buy, increasing the uncertainty of our model, but we can make a fair assumption that for large enough sample size the variance would cancel out errors, thus as e-commerce becomes more widely adopted this model would get more accurate, which is not the case for many models that use an empirical datasets or top-down approach for finding their abatement potential as it is only accurate for past trends.
